# Learning Path: Product Management for AI Engineers

**Target Audience**: Software Developer with AI Background
**Goal**: Transition to Product Management (PM) / AI Product Management
**Last Updated**: 2026-01-21

---

## 1. Executive Summary
This learning path is designed to leverage your existing technical and AI strengths while filling the gaps in business strategy, user psychology, and product discovery. As an engineer, you already understand *feasibility*; the goal is to master *desirability* (user needs) and *viability* (business value).

**Your Unfair Advantage**:
*   Deep understanding of ML capabilities and constraints.
*   Ability to communicate effectively with engineering teams.
*   "Technical Product Manager" (TPM) or "AI Product Manager" roles often pay a **20-25% premium**.

---

## 2. Prerequisites & Foundation
*   **Mindset Shift**: Move from "How do I build this?" to "Should we build this, and why?"
*   **Core Reading**: *Inspired* by Marty Cagan (The Bible of PM).

---

## 3. Phase 1: Core PM Fundamentals (Weeks 1-4)

### Week 1-2: Product Discovery & User Research
Focus on understanding the user problem before thinking about the solution.
*   **Concepts**: User Personas, Empathy Mapping, Jobs To Be Done (JTBD).
*   **Action**: Read *Continuous Discovery Habits* by Teresa Torres.
*   **Framework**: **JTBD** (Jobs To Be Done) - Focus on the outcome the user wants, not the feature.

### Week 3-4: Product Strategy & Roadmapping
Learn to prioritize effectively.
*   **Concepts**: Vision vs. Strategy vs. Roadmap, Prioritization Frameworks.
*   **Action**: Read *The Lean Product Playbook* by Dan Olsen.
*   **Frameworks**:
    *   **RICE** (Reach, Impact, Confidence, Effort) for quantitative prioritization.
    *   **MoSCoW** (Must, Should, Could, Won't) for requirements management.

---

## 4. Phase 2: Execution & Delivery (Weeks 5-8)

### Week 5-6: Agile, Methodologies & Design
You know Agile from the dev side; now learn it from the product owner side.
*   **Concepts**: Sprint Planning, Backlog Grooming, Writing User Stories, Acceptance Criteria.
*   **Action**: Take a structured course (e.g., **Product School** or **Udacity**).
*   **Key Skill**: Writing clear, unambiguous PRDs (Product Requirements Documents).

### Week 7-8: Metrics & Data-Driven Decision Making
*   **Concepts**: KPIs, OKRs, North Star Metric, A/B Testing.
*   **Action**: Read *Lean Analytics*.
*   **Framework**: **North Star Metric** - Aligning the team on a single long-term success indicator.

---

## 5. Phase 3: Advanced Topics & AI Specialization (Weeks 9-12)

### Week 9-10: AI Product Management
Your specialization zone.
*   **Focus**: Managing probabilistic products, data strategy, ethical AI, model lifecycle.
*   **Reading**:
    *   *The AI Product Playbook* by Dr. Marily Nika.
    *   *Designing Machine Learning Systems* by Chip Huyen.
*   **Certification**: Consider **IBM AI Product Manager** (Coursera) or **Stanford's Product Management in the Era of AI**.

### Week 11-12: Leadership & Career Growth
*   **Focus**: Stakeholder management, influence without authority.
*   **Action**: Listen to **Lenny's Podcast** (Strategy episodes).
*   **Community**: Join **Mind the Product** Slack or **Technical Product Manager** LinkedIn group.

## 7. PM Frameworks Reference
*   **Prioritization**: RICE, Kano Model, MoSCoW.
*   **Strategy**: SWOT, Lean Canvas, Blue Ocean Strategy.
*   **Problem Solving**: CIRCLES (Crucial for interviews).
*   **Metrics**: HEART (Google's UX framework), AARRR (Pirate Metrics).

---

## 8. Assessment & Milestones
*   **Month 1**: Can you articulate a product vision and create a basic roadmap using RICE?
*   **Month 2**: Can you write a comprehensive PRD and run a mock sprint planning session?
*   **Month 3**: Can you define the "North Star" for an AI product and explain the data strategy?
*   **Final Goal**: Complete a portfolio project (e.g., a PRD for a new AI feature in an existing product) and start applying for "Technical Product Manager" or "AI Product Manager" roles.
